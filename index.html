Hi, welcome to the troubleshooting guide.

Start

Do you have an NA or NB Miata (1990-2004) that's not starting?

Yes No

This guide is designed for NA and NB Miatas. It may or may not work for later years. Proceed at your own risk.

Proceed

First, check for anything glaringly obvious. Ensure your air intake is clear of blockages, check for catastrophic fluid leaking, disconnected grounds, etc.

Proceed

When you turn the key, what happens?

Nothing, or a single click then nothing. The starter cranks but the engine does not start.

Check your battery. Have it load tested at an auto-parts store. Ensure the terminals are clean and snug.

Check your starter. Ensure it is receiving power and listen for the solenoid clicking.

Check your ignition switch. Ensure the connections are clean.

When the key is turned to "RUN" does the Check Engine light come on?

Yes No

Pull the stored fault codes from the ECU and decipher them. Do you have any new/relevant codes?

Yes No

Address the code(s) you are seeing.

The ECU is not firing the Check Engine light. Is power hitting terminal 1A of the ECU?

Yes No

Check the Main EFI 30A fuse in the underhood fuse/relay box. Is it good?

Yes No

Remove the access panel under the driver side dash and locate the fuse box. Check the 10A “ROOM” fuse in the bottom left of the box. Is it good?

Yes No

You may have faulty wiring somewhere between the battery and the ECU.

Replace the fuse with a 10A fuse from any hardware store.

Check the Main EFI Relay. Turn your key to “RUN” and unplug/replug the relay to see if it clicks.

Strong click Weak click No click

Replace the EFI 30A fuse. I believe it is pink.

Check for continuity by applying +12v to terminal A, grounding terminal B and checking for continuity between terminals C and D. This procedure is detailed in the Enthusiast's Manual, page 5:42. Is it good?

Yes No

Replace the relay. Note that this is a dealer part.

Check for +12V at terminal 1B of the ECU with the key turned to "RUN". Is there voltage?

Yes No

Your ECU might be bad. If possible, test with a known good unit. Replace if necessary.

You may have a bad connection or faulty wiring somewhere in the circuit. Good luck, friend.

Remove your oil cap and watch your cam spin while a helper cranks the motor. Is your cam spinning (not just flopping around)?

Yes No

Your timing belt is likely broken. Examine it and replace if needed.

Check for spark at each cylinder. Do you see good strong spark?

Yes No

Ensure your spark plugs are in working order. They should be clean, free of oil, and should have acceptable gaps. Are they good?

Yes No

Replace your spark plugs with a known good set.

Turn the key to "RUN" and check for voltage at the positive terminal of the ignition coil connector. Good?

Yes No

You'll need to check the wiring, we've already ruled out the fuses and the ECU. Good luck.

Ensure your spark plug cables are good. Test with a known good car. Are they good?

Yes No

Check the resistances of the coils. The specifications are:

  • (1)Primary Coil: .78-.94 Ω
  • (2)Secondary Coil: 11.2-15.2 kΩ
  • (3)Primary Terminal to Case (insulation check): greater than 10M Ω

Replace your spark plug cables. Ensure you install them in the correct order.
